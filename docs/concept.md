# Harbor of Echoes Concept

## Tagline

The sea kept the sound. The town buried the rest.

## High-Level Pitch

`Harbor of Echoes` is a narrative investigation webapp set in a storm-beaten coastal town. The player acts as an external analyst with restricted access to a live case archive. Instead of following a fixed sequence, the player examines fragmented evidence, builds relationships between entities, and tries to reconstruct the truth behind a disappearance that may involve corporate misconduct, local loyalties, and manipulated records.

## Case Premise

Mara Vale, an environmental investigator, disappears shortly before she is due to publish findings linked to Blackwake Energy, the company behind the town's tidal infrastructure. Local authorities label the situation unresolved and politically sensitive. The player is granted access to a partial archive containing field notes, security records, personal messages, witness statements, and internal company documents.

The central question is not only "what happened to Mara?" but also:

- What was she about to reveal?
- Who had motive to stop her?
- Which records are trustworthy?
- Which timeline actually fits the evidence?

## Tone

- Cold, maritime, industrial
- Investigative rather than action-heavy
- Uneasy ambiguity instead of instant certainty
- Editorial and cinematic presentation

## Core Player Loop

1. Review newly unlocked evidence
2. Inspect details and metadata
3. Pin items to the case board
4. Connect evidence to people, places, and events
5. Detect contradictions and missing gaps
6. Build or revise hypotheses
7. Unlock more material through progress gates
8. Submit a final report

## Main Systems

### Evidence Vault

Archive of all discovered materials with filters for:

- type
- source
- date
- confidence
- tags
- related entities

Supported evidence examples:

- photos
- text messages
- emails
- interview transcripts
- incident reports
- CCTV stills
- access logs
- technical documents

### Case Board

A freeform visual workspace where the player can:

- pin evidence and entities
- draw manual links
- annotate assumptions
- group clusters by theory
- highlight contradictions

### Timeline

A chronological reconstruction layer showing:

- confirmed events
- uncertain events
- conflicting statements
- gaps in time

### Entity Profiles

Pages for suspects, witnesses, organizations, and locations. Each profile should expose links to:

- known relationships
- related evidence
- timeline appearances
- motives or conflicts

### Hypothesis Lab

A structured space where the player assembles theories with:

- claim
- supporting evidence
- contradictory evidence
- unresolved gaps
- confidence rating

## Initial Cast

- `Tomas Reed`: harbor master with incomplete statements
- `Elena Voss`: Blackwake Energy operations director
- `Jonah Quill`: local journalist chasing the same story
- `Iris Fen`: fisherman who reports a late-night sighting
- `Deputy Soren Pike`: officer controlling archive access

## Key Locations

- tidal plant
- old harbor
- cliff path
- lighthouse road
- Mara's rented flat
- records office

## Strong Product Direction

This should feel like a premium investigation interface, not a generic admin dashboard. The UI needs to support:

- dense but readable information
- artifact-like documents
- visual tension and atmosphere
- gradual narrative revelation

## MVP Definition

The first playable version should include:

- one case only
- one ending flow
- core archive browsing
- interactive case board
- timeline reconstruction
- final theory submission
