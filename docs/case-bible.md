# Harbor of Echoes Case Bible

## Canonical Case Title

`Case 01: The Vale Disappearance`

## One-Sentence Summary

Environmental investigator Mara Vale uncovered falsified structural data tied to Blackwake Energy's tidal array and was unintentionally killed during a covert confrontation staged to silence her, after which the town's power structure manipulated the record to protect the company and themselves.

## True Solution

Mara Vale discovered that Blackwake Energy had suppressed evidence of dangerous vibration faults in one of its tidal turbines. The flaw had already caused marine die-offs and threatened a catastrophic mechanical failure near the harbor channel. She planned to hand the data to journalist Jonah Quill and a national regulator.

Elena Voss, Blackwake's operations director, learned Mara had copied internal documents. Fearing a shutdown, criminal liability, and the collapse of the town's economy, she arranged a late-night meeting at the old harbor through Tomas Reed, the harbor master. The intent was intimidation and recovery of Mara's data drive, not murder.

Deputy Soren Pike, who had quietly assisted Blackwake in burying prior safety complaints, joined the confrontation to ensure Mara would not reach federal investigators. During the argument on the cliff path above the storm channel, Pike tried to seize Mara's satchel. She broke free, slipped on the wet stone, struck the barrier post, and fell onto a lower maintenance ledge. She survived the fall briefly but was critically injured.

Instead of calling emergency services, Pike and Tomas moved her bag, removed her recorder, and staged the scene to imply voluntary disappearance. Elena authorized deletion of access records inside the plant and the replacement of one inspection log. Mara later died from her injuries before dawn.

No single participant intended the exact outcome, but all three chose concealment over rescue. That moral failure is the core truth of the case.

## Thematic Spine

- profit versus public safety
- local loyalty versus truth
- institutional rot hidden behind economic dependence
- ambiguity in motive but clarity in responsibility

## Final Player Conclusion

The strongest end-state for the player is:

- Mara did not run away
- Mara was close to exposing real evidence
- Blackwake falsified safety reporting
- Pike obstructed the investigation
- Tomas helped cover up the aftermath
- Elena directed the institutional concealment
- Mara's death was caused by a coerced confrontation and the decision not to save her

## Character Breakdown

### Mara Vale

- role: environmental investigator
- public image: stubborn outsider
- private reality: methodical, isolated, increasingly alarmed
- goal: force disclosure before turbine activation review
- hidden detail: stored duplicate files in a dead-drop location

### Elena Voss

- role: operations director at Blackwake Energy
- public image: polished executive keeping the town employed
- private reality: knows the structural data was manipulated
- goal: prevent regulatory intervention and save the project
- line she will not cross: she does not plan a murder, but enables the cover-up once it happens

### Deputy Soren Pike

- role: deputy officer and archive gatekeeper
- public image: practical local authority
- private reality: tied to Blackwake through favors and concealed reports
- goal: keep outside investigators away from anything that reaches his desk
- hidden detail: destroyed one prior complaint months before Mara vanished

### Tomas Reed

- role: harbor master
- public image: tired civic loyalist holding the port together
- private reality: financially exposed if Blackwake collapses
- goal: recover Mara's copied files and avoid scandal
- hidden detail: moved evidence after the fall and lied about his route that night

### Jonah Quill

- role: local journalist
- public image: nuisance reporter
- private reality: one of the few people Mara trusted
- goal: publish once he has enough to survive legal pressure
- hidden detail: received only part of Mara's package before she disappeared

### Iris Fen

- role: fisherman
- public image: unreliable witness with a drinking reputation
- private reality: saw more than she realized and fears retaliation
- goal: stay out of town politics
- hidden detail: her original statement mentioned two voices, but the official copy removed one

## Timeline

### 10 Days Before Disappearance

- Mara receives an anonymous maintenance snapshot showing vibration anomalies in Turbine 3.
- She begins requesting environmental impact records from the local office.

### 8 Days Before

- Jonah Quill meets Mara at the records office cafe.
- Mara says she believes the published marine safety data is incomplete.

### 7 Days Before

- Mara interviews dock workers about restricted night maintenance runs.
- Deputy Pike flags her access requests as disruptive.

### 6 Days Before

- Mara photographs dead fish and unusual foam near the overflow channel.
- Blackwake issues an internal alert instructing staff not to discuss turbine servicing.

### 5 Days Before

- Mara gains access to a contractor laptop image through an unnamed source.
- She discovers two versions of a structural inspection report.

### 4 Days Before

- Jonah agrees to hold publication until Mara confirms chain of custody.
- Elena Voss learns from internal chatter that someone copied plant records.

### 3 Days Before

- Pike visits Tomas and warns that outside regulators are asking questions.
- Mara leaves a voicemail saying she finally has "the piece that proves intent."

### 2 Days Before

- Mara hides a duplicate evidence packet in a dead-drop locker near the old ferry terminal.
- Tomas sends Mara a message asking to meet about "something you need to hear before you publish."

### Night of Disappearance

- 20:10: Mara leaves her flat with satchel, recorder, and phone.
- 20:42: CCTV catches her near the harbor service road.
- 21:03: Tomas enters the old harbor perimeter.
- 21:11: Pike's patrol car is seen near lighthouse road, contradicting his later statement.
- 21:16: Mara meets Tomas near the cliff path access gate.
- 21:20: Elena calls Tomas for forty-seven seconds.
- 21:24: Pike arrives on foot from the upper road.
- 21:26: confrontation escalates when Pike demands the satchel and recorder.
- 21:28: Mara falls over the storm barrier onto the maintenance ledge below.
- 21:31 to 21:40: Tomas and Pike recover the satchel, remove the recorder, and debate calling for help.
- 21:43: Elena receives confirmation that "the situation is contained."
- 22:02: one plant access log entry is deleted.
- 22:18: Mara's phone stops transmitting.

### Following Morning

- Pike files Mara as missing rather than injured.
- The official search begins too late and in the wrong area.
- Jonah receives an incomplete encrypted package scheduled by Mara the previous day.

### 3 Days After

- Blackwake announces temporary maintenance due to weather stress.
- Iris attempts to revise her witness statement but is discouraged.

## Physical Truth of the Scene

- Mara fell from the upper cliff path to a narrow maintenance ledge
- the fall was accidental, but caused by coercive force in a hostile confrontation
- her satchel was removed from the original scene
- her recorder was taken and later damaged
- rescue was intentionally delayed until survival became impossible

## Misleading Theories Available to the Player

### Theory A: Mara Faked Her Own Disappearance

Why it seems plausible:

- satchel contents are missing
- messages suggest fear and secrecy
- she created a dead-drop

Why it fails:

- timeline evidence places her at a live confrontation
- injuries and scene traces contradict a voluntary escape

### Theory B: Tomas Acted Alone

Why it seems plausible:

- he arranged the meeting
- he lied repeatedly
- he moved physical evidence

Why it fails:

- Pike's patrol movement and archive tampering are separate
- Elena's call and altered plant logs show wider coordination

### Theory C: Elena Ordered a Direct Killing

Why it seems plausible:

- she had the clearest motive
- she approved concealment afterward

Why it fails:

- the evidence supports intimidation and forced recovery, not a premeditated hit
- the death results from escalation and deliberate failure to rescue

## Narrative Endings

### Strong Ending

Player identifies the combined responsibility of Elena, Pike, and Tomas, and correctly frames the death as an accidental fall turned fatal through conscious obstruction and cover-up.

### Partial Ending

Player proves Blackwake falsified data but misattributes the death to either suicide or planned murder.

### Weak Ending

Player concludes Mara ran away or treats the event as an isolated accident without institutional context.
